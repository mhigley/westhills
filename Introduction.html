<>

Every declaration and tag is surrounded by these guys.

In math they're the less than and greater than signs. In markup some people call them carrots, others call them angle brackets. Regardless, every tag needs them. Think of them as the bread that holds your sandwich together. Without them, it's just a royal mess.

Open and Close Tags

In addition to every tag needing to be wrapped in angle brackets (<>), most tags come in corresponding pairs.

For instance. You want to properly markup a paragraph. A paragraph tag would be ideal and it comes in two pieces: an opening <p> and a closing </p>.

<p>Your scientists were so preoccupied with whether or not they could, they never stopped to think if they should.</p>

Pay close attention to the differences between the open tag and the closing tag.

Void or Self-Closing Tags

There are a few exceptions to the open and closing tag rule. Some tags cannot have content. You saw that the <p> could contain text.

An image tag however cannot.

<img src="/img/classy_raptor.jpg" alt="A raptor wearing a top hat and bow tie">

Although there is a lot going on inside this tag which we'll get into, note that there is no closing tag.

Validation

https://validator.w3.org/nu/#textarea

You're going to want to bookmark this website. It validates your page and gives you valuable feedback to writing good markup.

Let's create the shortest possible valid website. You may not know what this is yet, but we'll review it shortly. For now, in your index.html write:

<!DOCTYPE html>

<title>World's Shortest Website</title>

Hello World
